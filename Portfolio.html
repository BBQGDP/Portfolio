In 2012, I gave my girlfriend (now ex-wife) her first laptop. With this new laptop, she started clicking all the links, which led to a network infestation of malicious software. One day I had noticed the network acting sluggish, but because of where we live, I assumed it was just poor-quality internet. The next day it got slower. The third day, my ex told me her laptop was acting funny. I asked her to show me, and to my surprise, it was almost non-functional. There were issues like the keyboard taking a few seconds to process every keystroke, the screen would randomly become pixelated, or artifact. I told her not to worry, and that I would fix it.

I researched her history and saw a few websites that stood out. I backed up all her files and asked her if she wanted anything in a specific place, like a folder on a USB drive to have ease of access to. After the backup was completed, I ran anti-virus software and used a program called DBAN to wipe her hard drive. Then I installed a fresh copy of Windows and made sure that worked properly. While I was performing the installation, I went into my router settings and started blacklisting the websites I found from her history. After the installation was completed, I had her create a new e-mail account, and set up junk folders for both of her e-mail accounts. I told her that her first e-mail was dead, and she could use that one to sign up for things online, but the new e-mail would be her professional/personal e-mail.
