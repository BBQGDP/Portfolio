Cover Letter

Hello, my name is Gregory “Dean” Perkins. I am interested in Rochester Institution of Technology because it offers a very in-depth cyber security program that also proclaims a 0% unemployment rate for students in this field. I am hoping to get the foundational knowledge for what it takes to be a Cyber Security analyst, and then get the knowledge to become an effective penetration tester. I am hoping the knowledge will equip me with the ability to perform these functions for the DoD. Hoping to be a penetration tester, I am really looking forward to learning compliance.

I have always had an affinity for computers. My father worked as a Network Administrator for the government, so I was constantly hearing about the non-technical parts of the jobs. He was constantly going out to conferences in other states, so I understand that continuing to grow is a huge part of the field. When we were in his truck going somewhere, he was constantly listening to audio books for certifications, so I was exposed to that at an early age.

As I spend more time in higher education, I am learning new and better security practices. As I learn new things about the technology I have at my disposal, I reduce my attack surface and increase my network hardening. I know these skills will translate to an enterprise in the future, so struggling now to ensure that my systems work will translate to less problems in the future when I am working on much larger systems.

I haven’t been in the field of Cyber Security, but I have a passion for knowledge. I know this career is growing and evolving. I currently have a 3.5GPA through two quarters, and I am hoping some classes will transfer over. I earned my SY0-601 CompTIA Security+ certification, as well as a Test Out Pro Security+ certification.

I feel I am a good fit for this program, because I grew up with computers, and want to maintain the highest security in all aspects of my life. There are so many programs your institution offers, and I think my skill set best fits a penetration tester. Your school also won the Collegiate Penetration Testing Competition in 2013. I think I would make a great addition to that team.

There was a gap between 2011 and 2022. I worked the occasional odd job but was mostly dealing with and fighting depression. At this point, I am on medication that has worked very well for me for the last 6 months, so this may be just the right combination to continue progressing.

I’ve only been in school for 6 months, but I pushed myself as hard as I could, and passed the CompTIA Security+. I am very passionate about growing and learning, and I look forward to hearing more about this specific application.
